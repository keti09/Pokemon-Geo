The Historic Region...

The second region to be intorduced in the core Pokémon games, Johto is based from the Kansai Region of Japan which includes historic areas such as Osaka and Kyoto, also the region where Nintendo HQ is located at. Like in real life Johto/Kansai is located west of the Kanto Region. Some big landmarks that inspire locations in this series were Meiji no Mori Minō Quasi-National Park, and the Expo Commemoration Park, which inspired the National Park area of the game. Just like how the Kansai has rich soil you can farm berries in the game something you weren't able to do in the previous games because of the volcanic soil that covered the Kanto region.

The Johto region is my favorite for it's rich history and lore with the Bell Tower and the Brass Tower being based off of Kinkaku-ji and Ginkaku-ji two temples in Kyto that have a silver and gold bird perched atop of them representing Lugia and Ho-oh respectively. Ercuteak City itself serving as a copy of Kyoto with its tradtional architecture and culture. The Ilex Forest in the games drew inspiration from Kumano Kodō, a series of ancient pilgrimage routes through a large and dense forest with the paths leading to a shrine. Which in the games housed the legendary Pokémon Celebi.

Johto Was the next region developed due to being the second largest region of Japan but also because of its rivalry with the Kanto region. Gold and Silver were intended to be the last pokemon games which is why developers went all out with content for this game. Another Legend is with the Burned Tower, which used to house the Legendary Pokemon Lugia but burned down due to a fire from a lightning storm which was then put out by the rain, 3 Pokémon died in the fire which were then revived by another Legendary Pokémon Ho-oh, resulting in the birth of Entei, Suicune, and Raikou.

Kinkaku-ji is covered in gold foil while its accompanying temple Ginkaku-ji was supposed to be covered in silver foil that never came to fruition unfortunately.
